How many times have you promised yourself you'd start exercising, spend less time on your phone, or finally tell your boss what you really think? And how many times did you find the perfect reason to put it off — "starting Monday," "once this is over," "when the time is right"?

## Why do we do what doesn't work for us?

The human brain is wired to avoid discomfort and seek immediate gratification. Change requires effort, and the status quo is comfortable — even when it makes us unhappy. Paradoxically, a familiar kind of suffering is often more appealing than an unfamiliar kind of change.

## How to break the cycle?

**Start small** — You don't have to change your whole life at once. One small step beats a thousand grand plans.

**Be gentle with yourself** — Self-criticism is not motivation. Understanding and accepting your own limitations is the first step toward change.

**Seek support** — Change is easier when you have someone who understands and encourages you. That could be a friend, a partner, or a psychotherapist.

Remember: starting is not the hard part. Continuing is. But it's precisely in that perseverance where real change lives.
