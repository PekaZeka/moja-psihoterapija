After decades of research, the renowned relationship scientist John Gottman identified four patterns of behaviour that predict the breakdown of a relationship with remarkable accuracy. He called them the "Four Horsemen of the Apocalypse."

## 1. Criticism

Criticism is not the same as a complaint. A complaint addresses a specific behaviour: "You didn't wash the dishes." Criticism attacks the person: "You never do anything — you're so irresponsible." Criticism erodes the sense of being accepted in a relationship.

## 2. Contempt

The most destructive of all four horsemen. Contempt involves belittling your partner — sarcasm, mockery, eye-rolling. When a partner feels contempt, the relationship is in serious danger.

## 3. Defensiveness

Instead of listening to our partner, we immediately defend ourselves and shift the blame. "I'm not the one at fault here — you started it!" Defensiveness blocks any progress in communication.

## 4. Stonewalling

A complete withdrawal from communication. One partner stops responding, "shuts down," and becomes emotionally unavailable. This is a sign that the person feels overwhelmed and needs a break.

## What can you do?

The good news is that these patterns can be recognised and changed. Through couples therapy, we work on developing healthier communication and re-establishing emotional connection.
