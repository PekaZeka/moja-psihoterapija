qa:
  - question: How do I know if we will break up?
    answer: >-
      John Gottman conducted longitudinal research with couples to identify
      predictors of relationship breakdown. Based on this, he defined the Four
      Horsemen of the Apocalypse: criticism (attacking the partner's character
      rather than a specific behavior), contempt (mockery, belittling, sarcasm,
      a sense of superiority), defensiveness (constant self-defense, blame-shifting,
      refusing responsibility), and stonewalling (emotional withdrawal and shutting
      down communication). If you recognize any of these patterns in your
      relationship, it is time to start working on them.
  - question: How do we stop arguing?
    answer: >-
      Arguments in themselves don't have to be destructive — even happy couples
      argue. However, they often spiral out of control and add toxicity to a
      relationship. Gottman's most famous finding is the 5:1 ratio: in stable
      couples, for every negative interaction during conflict, there are five
      positive ones — humor, touch, acknowledgment, accepting some responsibility,
      showing understanding. Negative interactions leave a stronger impression and
      linger longer, so the positive must predominate. A key shift in therapy is
      often changing the lens through which partners see each other — a lens that,
      over time, darkens and interprets everything negatively. Changing that
      perspective is the prerequisite for partners to step out of their trenches
      and reconnect.
  - question: How do I end a relationship I no longer want?
    answer: >-
      Many clients come to me with a desire to leave a relationship that is
      suffocating them — one with violence, obsessive control, or toxic patterns
      with no will from both partners to change. People stay for many reasons:
      insecurity, lack of support, fear of judgment, financial constraints. Those
      who stay for a long time forget themselves and their own strengths. The
      first step is returning to yourself — asking what you truly want and what
      your needs are, then building strength to reach a position where you can
      freely choose. And if you choose to stay, let it be because you want to,
      not because you see no way out.
  - question: How are family crises overcome?
    answer: >-
      Family crises typically arise during transitional phases — when a couple
      starts living together, when they have children, or when children grow up
      and leave home. These changes require adjustment from all family members.
      Extended families interfere in spouses' dynamics, parents unconsciously
      sabotage their children's independence. A systemic approach helps clients
      navigate these crises by becoming more aware of the problems, accepting new
      roles, and establishing a new structure. This therapy can be conducted with
      the whole family or with individual members.

Families and couples inevitably go through different phases, and the changes they bring are challenging and turbulent. Birth of a child, moving, children starting school, children leaving home, retirement, death of a family member... All these changes require life reorganization, stepping out of the comfort zone, renegotiating and compromising, which raises tension and brings plenty of reasons for arguments.

Moreover, our emotions change over time. Passion fades and sometimes falls short of expectations. It is deeply disappointing to notice that your partner no longer looks at you with the same intensity as before. One of the greatest crises is caused by infidelity, which tremendously burdens couples. Yet, practice shows that with an adequate approach, such breaks can be an opportunity to completely redefine the partnership and start anew.

Some choose divorce, which can also have various consequences. If you do decide to take this step, therapy can help you redefine family relationships, accept and bear the changes, and reorganize your life so that you begin to feel fulfilled and accomplished again.
