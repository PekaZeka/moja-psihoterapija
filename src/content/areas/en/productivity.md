qa:
  - question: How do I overcome laziness?
    answer: >-
      We are all lazy. Our brains evolved to conserve energy so we could survive
      more easily in scarce conditions. The point is not to despair about our
      laziness, but to organize our lives so that the easiest choices are the
      right ones. For example, if I want to use my phone less, I should not keep
      it next to my bed; if I want to eat healthily, I should not keep a drawer
      full of sweets at home. This is called choice architecture, and it is
      exactly what we can learn and practice in therapy.
  - question: How can I stay motivated all the time?
    answer: >-
      It is hard to be motivated all the time, and it is completely normal to have
      bad days. More important than motivation is discipline — a routine that
      exists even on slower days. This is achieved by building habits. Try to
      always practice a new habit at a similar time, ideally in the first part of
      the day, and link it to an existing habit — for example, meditating right
      after brushing your teeth. Other tricks for building new habits can be
      explored in therapy.
  - question: How can I be more productive?
    answer: >-
      Deep work techniques can help — described by Cal Newport in his bestseller.
      They are based on the idea that a person organizes a part of their day with
      no interruptions (no phones, no social media) and enters a state of deep
      focus where they can accomplish in a short time what they have been
      unsuccessfully trying to do for days while being constantly distracted. In
      therapy, you can adopt these techniques to increase your productivity and
      sense of satisfaction with what you achieve.

Achieving success in your work requires much more than talent and knowledge. It requires dedication, focus on goals, discipline, perseverance... For all of this, you need to master yourself — that is the ultimate goal that brings success in any area.

It's not hard to get motivated and start — it's much harder to maintain continuity and be consistent for as long as it takes to achieve the desired results.

The deep work approach can help — an excellent and empirically proven way to maximize your productivity by eliminating all distractions and reaching a state of complete focus.

Psychotherapy can help you in your development, setting goals, creating strategies and tactics for achieving them, and maintaining focus.

Through achieving these goals, you will grow stronger, build self-confidence, acquire skills, and empower yourself to independently solve new challenges and achieve new goals that you set for yourself.
