One of the most important books about couples psychotherapy is called "Love Is Never Enough" by Aaron Beck. He must have been heartless and insensitive to write such a terrible sentence... How is it possible that something as powerful as love is not enough?

Love is undoubtedly the most powerful force in the world — the one that drives it, sustains it, and gives it meaning. Yet, if we look around, we see so many couples who loved each other deeply but didn't make it, so many loves that hurt, so many wonderful people who love each other but can't stop hurting one another, so many loves that turned to contempt. It seems Beck knew what he was talking about after all.

Partner relationships are not just love — they are, as the name suggests, partnership, along with understanding, cooperation, compromise, alliance, exchange, communication, and much more. Marriage or partnership in any form needs nurturing, changes, goes through different phases, inevitably including crises that can be terribly painful. By working on all these elements, we can protect love or reawaken it and revive the most beautiful feelings that made us decide to spend our lives with the person we fell in love with.
